This one, in particular, is a ''super-massive black hole''. It is more than a ''billion'' times the mass of our Sun, and the black hole's 'radius', known as its event horizon, stretches out more than a hundered times the distance from Earth to the Sun. From behind this event horizon not even light can escape, but this doesn't mean that objects around it are instantly gobbled up by the black hole. In fact, they orbit in what's called an ''accretion disk'', getting extremely hot, and glowing with that heat, before spiralling into the centre. Because the gravity in the surrounding area is so strong, the light emitted on the far side of our view gets curved around the black hole. The accretion disk behind the black hole appears above and below the event horizon, and gives this mystifying shape.
